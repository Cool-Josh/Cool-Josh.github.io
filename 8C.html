HOW TO START PLAYING




Magic: the Gathering (Mtg) is a collectable card game released by Wizards of the Coast in 1993. This site is designed to inform prospective Mtg players about the various ways to play mtg, and where they can find them. Note that this site is not intended to teach players how to play the game, only to explain the different outlets through which a player can do so.

If you don't already know how to play the game, click the link below first.

Learn how to play here.

Before you can play Mtg, you must first choose a format. Formats are a designated set of rules and card lists that determine how the deck is constructed and how the game is played. Constructed formats involve assembling a deck prior to play, where as limited formats involve opening booster packs and making a deck on the spot.

Below is a basic overview of the most popular formats to play Mtg, including the general requirements, beginner friendliness, and where to find others to play that format.

CONSTRUCTED FORMATS

Standard

  • 2 players
  • 60 card minimum deck: No more than 4 copies of each card (excluding basic lands)
  • 20 starting life
  • Rotating format consisting only of cards printed in the last two years
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) and Magic the Gathering: Arena (MTGA), or in-person at a local game store or sactioned event
  • Beginner friendly
  • Rules

Modern

  • 2 players
  • 60 card minimum deck: No more than 4 copies of each card (excluding basic lands)
  • 20 starting life
  • Non-rotating format consisting only of cards printed since 2003
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) or in-person at a local game store or sactioned event
  • Not beginner friendly
  • Rules

Legacy

  • 2 players
  • 60 card minimum deck: No more than 4 copies of each card (excluding basic lands)
  • 20 starting life
  • Non-rotating format consisting of cards throughout Mtg's entire history
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) or in-person at a local game store or sactioned event
  • Experienced players only
  • Rules

Commander (also reffered to as EDH)

  • 3-4 players
  • 99 card minimum deck: No more than 1 copy of each card (excluding basic lands)
  • 40 starting life
  • Non-rotating format consisting of cards throughout Mtg's entire history
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) or in-person at a local game store
  • Beginner friendly
  • Rules

LIMITED FORMATS

Sealed

  • 2 players
  • 40 card minimum deck created using cards opened from 6 sealed 15-card booster packs
  • 20 starting life
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) and Magic the Gathering: Arena (MTGA) or in-person at a local game store
  • Beginner friendly
  • Rules

Draft

  • 2 players
  • 40 card minimum deck created using cards drafted from 3 sealed 15-card booster packs
  • Drafting involves players taking turns picking cards from booster packs
  • 20 starting life
  • Can be played digitally on Magic the Gathering: Online (MTGO) and Magic the Gathering: Arena (MTGA) or in-person at a local game store
  • Beginner friendly
  • Rules
